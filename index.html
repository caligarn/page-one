02 — Session 1 Recap

What We Covered

Tuesday, March 3, 2026 — the inaugural session. The first time the full cohort was together at Digital Garage.

Program Overview & Orientation

Minh walked the cohort through the full slide deck covering the program's structure, timeline, and philosophy. He emphasized that this is a pilot program being built in collaboration with the cohort, not a top-down curriculum. The six-to-seven-week roadmap was presented: foundation and ideation in week one, development in week two, pitch prep in week three, refinement in week four, final polish in week five, final rehearsal in week six, and Demo Day in week seven.

The schedule was established as Tuesdays in-person (6–9 PM) and Thursdays online (6–7:30 PM), though the first Thursday would need to shift to Monday due to a scheduling conflict.

What the Program Is and Isn't

Minh was transparent about what the accelerator can and cannot offer. It is not a funding source, a production service, a classroom, or a guarantee of distribution. It is a structured creative environment with mentorship, tool access, and a pathway to present finished work to industry professionals. Participants are expected to drive their own projects and show up ready to make things between sessions.

Tools, Mentorship & Partner Access

The program's partner network was presented, including tool credit sponsors such as Runway, Pika, ElevenLabs, Midjourney, Adobe, Luma AI, NVIDIA, Rosebud, Koyal, Popcorn, Descript, Reve, and others. Minh mentioned that Rosebud would be coming to a future session to demo their platform for anyone interested in interactive/game-adjacent work. Participants were invited to apply directly for tools like InVideo Art and to let Minh know what they need.

A quick show of hands revealed the cohort ranges from beginner to advanced in AI tool proficiency, with most falling in the intermediate-to-advanced range.

Business & Legal Discussion

This was one of the most substantive conversations of the evening. Minh walked through the legal landscape transparently, including the lawyer's recommendation for LLC formation, the standard accelerator equity model (3–10%, with Minh initially thinking around 5%), and the complications specific to content IP versus traditional startup equity.

The group discussed multiple scenarios: whether individuals should create their own LLCs, whether a single cohort-level entity could house all projects with universal licensing carve-outs (so creators don't feel locked in), and what happens if one project becomes a major hit. Adam raised the point that $1,000 for an LLC is a significant ask when the IP value is still uncertain.

Minh also shared that he has been in conversation with people involved in recent San Francisco legislation offering a 35% rebate on project spending, and is exploring whether grant money could flow into a program like this. Nothing is finalized — all terms will be handled with full transparency and explicit consent.

The discussion also covered AI copyrightability, referencing the Supreme Court's recent decision to decline hearing a case about AI-generated IP. Minh's advice: document everything. Record brainstorms, save prompts, keep notes, even video yourself during creative sessions. This paper trail of human creative involvement is what has worked in the few successful cases so far.

Participant Expectations

Minh outlined what the program asks of participants: show up to the weekly sessions (both in-person and online), do the creative work between sessions, give honest feedback to cohort members and to the program itself, be generous with collaboration (low ego, free feedback culture), and communicate when something isn't working. He emphasized that this is a community, not a competition.

Creator Introductions (Rounds 1 & 2)

The bulk of the evening was dedicated to introductions. Each participant took 2–5 minutes to share who they are, what project they're bringing to the accelerator, what medium they're working in, and what they need to succeed. Several participants showed visual work — trailers, concept art, demos, and music.

Presentations were split across two halves: the first batch presented before a food break, and the remaining presenters continued after. Adam proposed a "speed jam" methodology for future sessions — a rapid collaborative creation process where the group generates content for someone's project in a focused 30-minute to 2-hour burst, then edits it down to see what works.

Communication & Logistics

A WhatsApp group was created for the cohort (preferred over Discord for ease of daily follow-up). All coordination, announcements, and updates will happen through the WhatsApp group. Minh committed to summarizing all project pitches from the session recordings and putting them into a shared document that everyone can review and edit.

Goal-Setting Preview & Close

Vesica suggested (drawn from a previous incubator experience) that the cohort do a public stating of goals in week two — e.g., "In six weeks I will have a 30-second video with 100 people having watched it." Minh agreed this was a great idea and said we'd formalize goal-setting in the shared document for next week. The session closed with a group photo.

04 — Roadmap

Week-by-Week
Milestones

Your compass for the next seven weeks. We'll adapt as we go, but this is the trajectory.

Week Theme What We're Doing Your Deliverable
WK 1 Foundation & Ideation Introductions, program overview, legal/business context, cohort bonding. Visual storytelling principles, lookbook inspiration. Decide on your project. Fill out the shared project document. Set your 6-week goal.
WK 2 Development Kickoff Guest tool demos. Deep-dive into AI creative workflows. Begin building your visual world and narrative structure. First visual asset or story outline. Lookbook / moodboard. Identify your collaborators.
WK 3 Pitch Prep Pitch structure workshop. How to tell your story in 5 minutes. Feedback rounds on early pitches. Draft pitch script or outline. 30-second teaser or concept trailer in progress.
WK 4 Refinement Story and worldbuilding polish. Advanced AI workflow sessions. 1-on-1 mentor check-ins. Revised pitch with visual assets. At least 1 episode / chapter / prototype section complete.
WK 5 Final Polish Full rehearsal of pitches. Peer feedback and iteration. Production quality review. Near-final pitch package. All episodes / assets in final draft.
WK 6 Final Rehearsal Tech check and run-through. Last mentor feedback. Final tweaks. Completed pitch package. Proof-of-concept ready to screen.
WK 7 Demo Day 🎬 Present to funders, producers, distributors, and industry leaders at Digital Garage. 5-minute live pitch. Screened proof-of-concept. Q&A with audience.

This roadmap is a guide, not a rigid schedule. We'll adapt based on how the cohort is progressing. If we need to extend beyond seven weeks, we will.
