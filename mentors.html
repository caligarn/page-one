01 — Welcome

What Is Page One?

Page One is a six-week pilot accelerator by Machine Cinema for creative AI projects. We're helping 17 creators go from concept to a pitchable proof-of-concept, culminating in a Demo Day in front of funders, studios, and distributors.

🎯

The Goal

Each participant is building a creative AI project — episodic series, feature films, interactive experiences, transmedia worlds, visual narratives. They need to leave with a pitch package and a proof-of-concept they can screen.

👥

The Cohort

17 creators from diverse backgrounds — filmmakers, writers, designers, musicians, technologists, producers. Skill levels with AI tools range from beginner to advanced. All are ambitious and self-directed.

📍

The Format

Tuesdays in-person (6–9 PM) at Digital Garage, SF. Thursdays online (6–7:30 PM). Mentors can join either or both — whatever works for your schedule.

🎤

Demo Day

Week 7. YC-style showcase — 5-minute pitch, screen proof-of-concept, Q&A. Audience includes film funders, studios, streaming platforms, and distributors. Mentors are invited to attend.

03 — Expectations

What We're Asking
of Mentors

There's no rigid commitment. We want mentorship to feel natural, not burdensome. Here's the spirit of what we're looking for.

Share What You Know

You're here because you have experience that matters — in storytelling, production, technology, business, distribution, or creative craft. The most valuable thing you can do is share that honestly. Our participants are not looking for hand-holding; they're looking for perspective from people who've been there.

Give Direct, Honest Feedback

These creators are building real projects on tight timelines. Encouraging but vague feedback doesn't help. What helps: "Your concept is strong, but your pitch buries the hook — lead with the conflict." Specificity is generosity. The cohort has explicitly opted into a culture of direct, low-ego feedback.

Be Available When You Can

We're not asking for weekly office hours or ongoing commitments unless you want that. Some mentors might join one session for a talk. Others might check in with a specific creator every couple of weeks. Some might do a one-off review of pitch decks. All of it helps. Tell us what works for you and we'll make it happen.

Respect the Creative Process

The participants own their projects and their IP. Mentorship in this program means guidance, not direction. The best mentors ask great questions ("What does success look like for you?"), offer frameworks ("Here's how I've seen pitches succeed"), and resist the urge to take over. These are their stories to tell.

Keep It Confidential

The project ideas, pitch materials, and creative work shared in this program are confidential. Please don't share specific project details or creator work outside the program without their explicit permission. This trust is what allows people to share openly and take creative risks.

04 — Schedule

Program Timeline

The week-by-week roadmap, so you know what the cohort is working on and when your expertise might be most useful.

Week Theme Focus Where Mentors Can Help
WK 1 Foundation & Ideation Introductions, project selection, goal-setting, legal/business context. Help participants choose between project concepts, sharpen their vision.
WK 2 Development Kickoff Tool demos, AI creative workflows, building visual worlds and narrative structure. Tool expertise, workflow advice, worldbuilding feedback.
WK 3 Pitch Prep Pitch structure, storytelling in 5 minutes, early pitch feedback rounds. Pitch coaching, story structure, presentation feedback.
WK 4 Refinement Story polish, advanced AI workflows, 1-on-1 check-ins. 1-on-1 mentoring sessions, production quality review.
WK 5 Final Polish Full pitch rehearsals, peer feedback, production quality review. Mock pitch audiences, detailed critique of near-final work.
WK 6 Final Rehearsal Tech check, run-throughs, last mentor feedback, final tweaks. Final pitch dry-run, last-mile production notes.
WK 7 Demo Day Present to funders, producers, distributors, and industry leaders. Attend Demo Day, provide industry connections, post-pitch introductions.

Sessions run Tuesdays 6–9 PM (in-person at Digital Garage, SF) and Thursdays 6–7:30 PM (online). You're welcome at any session — just let us know you're coming.

05 — Get Involved

Ways to
Participate

There's no one-size-fits-all for mentorship. Here are concrete ways you can contribute — pick what fits your expertise and availability.

🎙️

Give a Talk

Lead a 20–45 minute session on something you know deeply. Can be a Tuesday evening or Thursday online. We'll handle logistics — you just show up and share.

  • AI tools and creative workflows
  • Pitching to studios, funders, or distributors
  • Production pipelines and post-production
  • IP strategy, business models, or distribution
  • Storytelling, worldbuilding, or narrative design
👥

Mentor a Small Group

Work with 2–4 participants who share a common challenge or medium. Meet once or twice over the program — in person or over video. Great if you want to go deeper than a single session.

  • Episodic content creators
  • Interactive/gaming projects
  • First-time AI filmmakers
  • Pitch and business strategy
  • Transmedia and worldbuilding
🔍

1-on-1 Reviews

Review a specific creator's pitch, concept, or proof-of-concept and give targeted feedback. This could be a single 30–60 minute conversation — sometimes that's all someone needs to break through.

  • Pitch deck review and critique
  • Story or concept feedback
  • Technical workflow advice
  • Industry-specific guidance
🌐

Demo Day & Beyond

Attend Demo Day (Week 7) as an audience member, panelist, or connector. If you know funders, distributors, or producers who should see this work, help us get them in the room.

  • Attend and ask questions at Demo Day
  • Make introductions to industry contacts
  • Offer post-program follow-up or advisory
